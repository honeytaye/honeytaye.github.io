03 — Projects

Project 01 · Automotive Analytics
Forecasting Clustering Classification Multi-Region

BMW Global Sales Analysis & Predictive Modeling (2010–2024)

Conducted a full-cycle data analysis engagement on 14 years of BMW vehicle sales data spanning six global regions — Africa, Asia, Europe, the Middle East, North America, and South America. The analysis addressed three core business questions through three distinct modeling approaches, delivering strategic intelligence to support market planning, product development, and customer targeting.

Sales Forecasting — Applied ARIMA time-series modeling to historical yearly totals, projecting 2025 global sales volume at 16.54 million units. The wide prediction interval surfaced significant market volatility, informing BMW's need for flexible production and supply chain planning.
Customer Segmentation — Deployed multi-feature K-Means clustering (K=3), encoding fuel type, transmission, region, and model to move beyond price-only segmentation. Identified three behaviorally distinct segments: traditional petrol drivers, diesel-hybrid transition customers, and full EV adopters — enabling targeted marketing by technology readiness rather than spend.
Sales Classification — Built a logistic regression classifier to determine whether vehicle specifications predict high sales volume. The 50.4% accuracy finding confirmed that individual vehicle attributes do not drive sales outcomes, redirecting strategic focus toward macroeconomic factors, competitor pricing, and regional advertising spend.

Project 02 · Energy Infrastructure Analytics
CRISP-DM SQL BI Dashboard Capacity Analysis

Trans Mountain Pipeline — Throughput & Capacity Intelligence

Delivered an end-to-end CRISP-DM data analysis on Trans Mountain Pipeline throughput and capacity records spanning 2006–2020. The engagement moved from business understanding through to visualization, applying the full six-phase methodology to surface operational insights for infrastructure decision-making.

Trade Type Analysis — Authored and executed SQL queries against a structured pipeline dataset to determine trade type prevalence. Analysis confirmed that export flows were the dominant trade type in the most recent year on record, accounting for 57% of total throughput (24 of 42 recorded trades in 2020).
Peak Capacity Identification — Queried and ranked annual available capacity across the full 15-year period. Identified 2019 as the peak year with a total capacity of 4,214 units — providing a data-grounded benchmark for infrastructure planning and expansion assessment.
Dashboard Delivery — Constructed Power BI dashboards presenting a pie chart of trade type distribution and a line chart of available capacity by year, enabling stakeholders to surface trends and support operational reporting without reliance on ad-hoc queries.
